Mahdieh Abbasi's Bio

I graduated with a PhD in Computer Engineering from Universite Laval (UL), researching under supervision of Christian Gagne (the director of Insitute Intelligence and Data and a MILA's professor) and Denis Laurendeau (the director of Computer Vision and System Lab). My Ph.D. research revolved around dependable and secure learning models to adversarial examples and Out-of-Distribution samples. Before joining UL, I was a researcher master student at Digital Media Lab of Sharif University of Technology (SUT), working on 3D human pose estimation under supervision of H. Reza. Rabiee. I obtained my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from SUT. As my BSc project, I studied and investigated the community detection algorithms for detecting communities of Iranian Mathematicians around the globe.

  • Adversarial Networks; Domain Adversarial Networks (DAN), Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN)
